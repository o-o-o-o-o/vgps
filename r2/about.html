Varese Group convened for the first time in 2017 when Fiona Connor invited a small group of artists, writers, architects, curators, and designers to meet in northern Italy, with a gentle commitment to keep coming back in the following years. For three consecutive summers we spent a full, unhurried week together, with each participant gently taking responsibility for an event or presentation: giving a talk or slide show, presenting artwork, leading a field trip. Common interests included a fascination with place, site, and temporality; a commitment to institutional critique; and a desire to delve into the histories of architecture and exhibition design. The materiality of a specific location was central to these concerns. We made food together, went on long walks, and discovered overlapping interests and intersecting histories. We made new friends and indulged old ones; certain individuals fell in love; coincidences unfolded. One year a baby attended, and we all discovered the meaning of tummy time. The participants came from Los Angeles, Auckland, Berlin, New York, Lisbon, Glasgow, various cities in Italy, and elsewhere. In time, a series of programs took place in Los Angeles, and during the pandemic, a fourth iteration of Varese Group came together remotely, with old and new participants spanning multiple countries and time zones.

The Varese Group Pamphlet Series was initiated as a way for the group to share threads of research and output with a broader public. Pamphlets are published in series, with the first set of three by Leslie Dick, Michael Ned Holte, and Warren Olds, followed by a second set by Marta Fontolan, Sasha Portis, and Lucy Skaer.

The pamphlets are Risograph-printed in black on 70 lb. White Cougar paper, and staple-bound in quarto signatures.
